3.4: Sound

  • (3.4.1) Sound Wave Features
    • Mechanism of Sound Waves
      • Sound waves are produced by vibrating sources (type of pressure wave)
        • Longitudinal nature; requires a medium
          • Compression: region of higher density
          • Rarefaction: region of lower density
    • Frequency & Pitch
      • Frequency (thus wavelength) affects pitch
        • High frequency = High pitch
        • Low frequency = Low pitch
    • Amplitude & Volume
      • Amplitude affects volume
        • Large amplitude = High volume
        • Small amplitude = Low volume
    • Echoes
      • Echoes are the reflection of sound waves (off hard surfaces)
        • Can be used to calculate water depth
        • Wave Speed ×{\times}×﻿ Time
  • (3.4.2) Sound Wave Facts
    1. Approximate range of frequencies audible to humans = 20 Hz to 20 000 Hz
    1. At RTP, sound waves travel at a speed of about 340 m/s
      • The speed of sound in air varies from 330-350 m/s
    1. In general, sound travels faster in solids than in liquids and faster in liquids than in gases
  • (3.4.3) Demonstrating the Speed of Sound
    1. Distance / Time; 2 points (100m or more)
    1. Distance / Time; echo (must be 50m or more)
    1. Oscilloscope & 2 microphones (5m apart) → time between peaks
  • (3.4.4) Ultrasounds
    • Ultrasound = sound with a frequency higher than 20,000 Hz (“transducers”)
