  • (2.2.2) Internal Energy
    • Internal Energy is the total energy stored inside a system by the particles that make up the system due to their motion and positions
      • Motion = kinetic energy
      • Positions = potential energy (attraction)
    • Increase in temperature of an object increases its internal energy
      • Due to an increase in the average kinetic energies of its particles

  • (2.2.4) Change of State
    • Melting & Boiling
      • Energy input without a change in temperature
        • Thermal energy is all transferred into overcoming intermolecular forces

          (endothermic)
    • Condensation & Solidification
      • Energy input without a change in temperature
        • Thermal energy is transferred from forming intermolecular bonds

          (exothermic)
    • Evaporation
      • Occurs when more energetic molecules moving near the surface of the liquid have enough energy to escape
        • At any temperature & only at the surface of a liquid (boiling is throughout)
        • Average energy of the liquid is reduced, and thus cooled
        • Objects in contact with an evaporating liquid also cools the object
      • Factors Affecting Evaporation
        1. Temperature: affects the average kinetic energy of the molecules in a liquid
        1. Surface area: affects the rate of evaporation
        1. Air movement over a surface: affects the concentration gradient

2.3: Transfer of Thermal Energy

(Most energy transfers have all three types of energy transfer occurring at the same time)

  • (2.3.1) Conduction
    • Mechanism of Conduction
      • Conduction occurs when two solids of different temperatures come in contact with one another and thermal energy is transferred from the hotter object to the cooler object
        • Particles bump into each other, transferring internal energy → until equilibrium
          • (a) Atomic/Molecular Lattice Vibrations
          • (b) Free/Delocalised Electron Collisions (in metallic conductors)
    • Conduction in Fluids
      • Thermal conduction is bad in:
        • Gases because the particles are too widely spread apart
        • Most liquids because the particles slide past each other
    • Investigating Conduction
      • Wax on ends of a cylinder of [half metal] [half glass]
        • Heat middle (metal-side will melt first)
  • (2.3.2) Convection
    • Mechanism of Convection
      • Method of thermal energy transfer in liquids and gases
        • Temperature affects density
      • The difference in density of an unevenly heated/cooled fluid causes:
        • Heated fluid to rise → burners
        • Cooled fluid to sink → air conditioner
        • Convection currents
    • Investigating Convection
      • Convection currents can be demonstrated using crystals of potassium permanganate
        • Purple; only dissolves in hot water
  • (2.3.3) Radiation
    • Mechanism of Infrared Radiation
      1. All objects give off thermal radiation
      1. Part of the electromagnetic spectrum (infrared radiation)
      1. Does not require a medium (can travel in a vacuum)
    • Thermal Equilibrium
      • As an object absorbs infrared radiation it becomes hotter
      • As an object becomes hotter it also emits more infrared radiation
      • Equilibrium is when it absorbs & emits radiation at the same rate (constant temp.)
        • Cooling down: an object transfers away energy at a higher rate than it receives it
        • Heating up: an object receives energy at a higher rate than it transfers it away
        • This process will always move towards thermal equilibrium
    • Factors of IR Absorption & Emission
      1. Surface Area (more area for radiation to be absorbed/emitted from)
      1. Surface Colour & Texture
        ColourAbsorptionEmission
        BlackGoodGood
        Dull/DarkReasonableReasonable
        WhitePoorPoor
        ShinyVery Poor (reflects)Very Poor
    • Demonstrating Radiation
      • Emitter: black & white container of hot water
        • (black container will cool first)
      • Absorber: 2 thermometers covered in black or white, around fire
        • (black thermometer shows hotter)
    • The Greenhouse Effect
      • The temperature of the Earth is affected by factors controlling the balance between incoming radiation and radiation emitted from the Earth’s surface
        • Earth gets radiation from the Sun and reflects most of it back
        • Gases in the atmosphere (greenhouse gases) absorb and reflect black longer-wavelength infrared radiation from the Earth and prevents heat from escaping into space (warming)
          • Water vapour
          • Methane
          • CO2
